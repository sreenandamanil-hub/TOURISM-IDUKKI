Welcome to Idukki Tourism

Welcome to Idukki, one of the most beautiful hill districts in Kerala. Known for its green mountains, cool climate, and peaceful atmosphere, Idukki is a perfect place for nature lovers. The district is full of forests, waterfalls, rivers, and wildlife. It is also home to the famous Idukki Arch Dam, one of the highest arch dams in Asia. Visitors can enjoy fresh air, scenic views, and a calm environment away from busy city life.

Idukki is a land of natural beauty and adventure. Popular places like Munnar, Ramakkalmedu, Vagamon, and Thekkady attract tourists from all over the world. Tea plantations, spice gardens, and wildlife sanctuaries make this place unique. Visitors can enjoy trekking, boating, and wildlife watching. The culture and traditions of the local people also add charm to this destination.

The history of Idukki is closely connected with its forests and tribal communities. In earlier times, the region was covered with dense forests and was home to many indigenous tribes. Later, during the British period, people started cultivating tea, coffee, and spices. The construction of the Idukki Dam in the 1970s was an important milestone, which helped in the development of the district.

Over the years, Idukki has grown into a major tourist destination while still preserving its natural beauty. The district was officially formed in 1972, and since then it has become known for eco-tourism. Even today, Idukki remains one of the least polluted and most peaceful places in Kerala, making it an ideal destination for relaxation and exploration.
